The Contortionist

The contortion is an acrobatic discipline practised in the circus, in gymnastics and in cabaret-variety. She is based on extreme movements of flexion and extension of the human body. She requires an important flexibility, an often natural physical capacity, associated with a length (several years) and requiring training. In some very rare cases, certain contortionists are reached of the Syndrome of Ehlers Danlos, a genetic disease which modifies the structure of the collagen and returns very elastic connective tissues.

The lion tamer

The term often applies to the taming and display of other big cats such as tigers, leopards, jaguars, cheetahs, and cougars. People often use lion taming as a metaphor for a dangerous activity, due to the obvious risks of toying with powerful, instinctive carnivores. Lion taming occurs in zoos across the world, to enable the keepers to carry out medical procedures and feedings. The Captive Animals Protection Society maintains that animal welfare cannot be guaranteed in circuses.
